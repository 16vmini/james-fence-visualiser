# Build plan — timber infill fence on the front wall

Two framed slatted panels sitting on top of the curved white wall, filling the
gaps between the three rendered piers. Flat top running post-top to post-top,
slat bottoms scribed to follow the wall's curve.

Approximate sizes scaled from the photo — **measure before buying anything**:

| | Width (between piers) | Height at piers | Height at centre of dip |
|---|---|---|---|
| Left panel | ~1.75 m | ~40 cm | ~60 cm |
| Right panel | ~1.45–1.60 m | ~35 cm | ~55 cm |

## 0. Check before you build (important)

- **Planning permission.** In England a wall/fence **next to a highway is limited
  to 1 m total height** under permitted development. The wall + piers are already
  around that, so adding fencing on top very likely needs a householder planning
  application. It's on a corner by a junction too, so highways visibility splays
  can come into it. Check with **Havant Borough Council** before spending money —
  a quick pre-application enquiry or a call to planning is cheap insurance.
- **Ownership/boundary** — confirm the wall is James's and not shared.

## 1. Measure and template

1. Pull a **string line** taut from the top of one pier to the next — this is the
   flat top line.
2. Every **30 cm** along, measure the drop from the string to the wall coping.
   Write them on masking tape stuck to the wall. This is the cutting list for
   the slat lengths.
3. Measure the true opening width between pier faces at the top and at the
   wall shoulder (piers aren't always plumb).

## 2. Materials (treated softwood throughout)

| Item | Size | Use | Qty (approx) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slats | 70 × 19 mm (or match whatever board width you liked in the visualiser) | Vertical boards | ~40–44 slats, avg 50 cm → ~12 × 1.8 m lengths |
| Rails | 45 × 34 mm (or 38 × 50) | 2 horizontal rails per panel | 4 lengths, ~1.8 m |
| Stainless angle brackets | heavy duty, 4 per panel | fix rails to pier faces | 8 |
| Frame fixings / sleeve anchors | 8 × 80 mm masonry, stainless or A2 | into piers | 16 |
| Stainless screws | 4 × 40 mm | slats to rails | ~180 |
| Exterior wood stain/paint | your chosen colour (golden oak etc.) | 2 coats all faces | 1 × 2.5 L |
| Clear silicone / packers | | drainage gap at wall interface | 1 tube |

Rough cost: **£130–180** in timber and fixings. Tools: drill (SDS or decent
hammer action for the piers), 6/8 mm masonry bits, mitre or circular saw, level,
string line, jigsaw if you scribe curves.

## 3. Build (per panel)

1. **Rails first.** Two straight rails per panel:
   - Top rail ~8 cm below the flat top line.
   - Bottom rail just **above the highest points of the wall curve** (near the
     piers) so it clears the coping everywhere — roughly 28–30 cm below the top.
2. Fix rails to the pier faces with stainless angle brackets. Drill the piers
   with care — if the render sounds hollow, use resin anchors instead of
   expanding ones so you don't blow the render off.
3. **Stain/paint everything before assembly** — especially end grain and the
   backs of slats. Two coats.
4. **Slats.** Work out spacing so the gaps come out even:
   `n = round((opening + gap) / (slat + gap))`, then nudge the gap so the last
   slat lands flush at the pier. Cut each slat to its measured length from step 1
   — square cut at the top (the flat line does the work), bottom cut to leave a
   **10 mm shadow gap above the render** so water drains and the render doesn't
   stain. For a smoother look, angle-cut each slat bottom a few degrees to
   follow the curve locally.
5. Screw slats to both rails, one stainless screw per rail, keeping tops dead on
   the string line. In the middle of the dip the slats hang ~20 cm below the
   bottom rail — fine at this size.
6. Touch up cut ends with stain.

## 4. Details that make it look right

- Keep every slat **plumb** (vertical), not square to the curve.
- A **flat top capping rail** (e.g. 70 × 19 laid flat) across the slat tops
  stiffens the panel and reads much more "finished" — optional but recommended.
- Don't bed timber directly on the coping: trapped water is what rots fences.
  The 10 mm gap is your friend.
- Stainless fixings only — galvanised will streak black down white render.

## 5. Time

Half a day to measure/buy, half a day per panel for a competent DIYer.
Staining before assembly is the slow part — do it the evening before.
